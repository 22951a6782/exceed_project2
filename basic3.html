Degree Levels Available: Bachelors, masters, Ph.D. Popular Specializations: Aeronautical engineering (working with aircraft), astronautical engineering (working with spacecraft) Common Career Paths: Aerospace engineer, mechanical engineer, drafter, inspector, compliance officer Major Overview: Aerospace engineering majors learn how to design, test and operate aircraft, spacecraft and other related technologies. Aerospace engineers create and develop these systems and their criteria for quality and safety. Most aerospace engineering roles require a bachelors degree. Those interested in teaching at the university level or conducting advanced research should hold a masters. Aerospace engineers bring home a median salary of $126,880 annually
